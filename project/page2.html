Discover Your Best Colors

Ready to find out which colors make you shine? This 3-step guide walks you through everything you need to know to discover your exact seasonal palette out of the 12 types.

Step 1: Understand the 3 Color Traits

1. Hue – Are You Warm or Cool?

This refers to the undertone of your features.
• Warm = yellow, golden, or peachy tones
• Cool = blue, pink, or rosy tones
Test: Do you look better in gold (warm) or silver (cool)?

2. Value – Are You Light, Medium, or Dark?

This refers to how light or deep your features are, especially hair and skin.
Test: Convert a selfie to black and white
• If everything looks pale = Light
• If your hair or eyes are much darker than your skin = Dark
• If it’s in between = Medium

3. Chroma – Are You Bright or Muted?

This refers to how clear or soft your overall coloring is.
Test: Hold up a bright pink top and a dusty rose top.
• Bright pink suits you better = Bright
• Dusty rose suits you better = Muted

Step 2: Figure Out Your Combination

Ask yourself:
• Which trait was the most obvious? = That’s your Primary Trait
• What came second? = That’s your Secondary Trait
• The remaining trait is your Tertiary Trait
This 3-part combination of your primary, secondary, and tertiary trait is the key to unlocking your exact color palette.

Step 3: Match Your 3-Part Combination to a Season

Bright Spring

Warm + Bright + Light

Fresh, vibrant colors like coral, warm turquoise, and lime

Avoid: muted, dull, or cool shades

True Spring

Warm + Bright + Medium

Clear, sunny colors like peach, goldenrod, and warm green

Avoid: anything cool or dusty

Light Spring

Warm + Muted + Light

Delicate pastels like mint, soft peach, and light apricot

Avoid: dark or highly saturated colors

Light Summer

Cool + Muted + Light

Soft pastels like powder blue, soft lilac, and blush

Avoid: warm, dark, or saturated tones

True Summer

Cool + Muted + Medium

Cool, gentle tones like rose, periwinkle, and dove gray

Avoid: warmth or high contrast

Soft Summer

Cool + Muted + Medium

Smoky colors like mauve, stone blue, and faded navy

Avoid: vivid or warm shades

Soft Autumn

Warm + Muted + Medium

Earthy, blended tones like sage, dusty rose, and camel

Avoid: icy or vibrant colors

True Autumn

Warm + Muted + Medium

Rich autumn tones like rust, mustard, and olive

Avoid: cool or bright colors

Dark Autumn

Warm + Muted + Dark

Deep, warm colors like espresso, forest green, and auburn

Avoid: light or icy shades

Bright Winter

Cool + Bright + Dark

High-contrast jewel tones like cobalt, fuchsia, and black

Avoid: muted or earthy colors

True Winter

Cool + Bright + Medium

Icy, striking shades like true red, electric blue, and white

Avoid: muted or warm tones

Dark Winter

Cool + Muted + Dark

Deep, intense tones like charcoal, plum, and burgundy

Avoid: pastels or warmth
